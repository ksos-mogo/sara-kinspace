The problem she's obsessed with: making sure people understand, and feel understood. The methodology has been the same for 14 years across every context: ask the question that finds what's been missed, then build from what you find.

Seeing what your position makes invisible

How you're showing up

A guided process of becoming more reflexive about your own patterns — how you respond under pressure, what you reach for when things get hard, what you do in conflict that you'd change if you could see it in real time. Pattern recognition that changes what happens next.

The question before the strategy

Every session runs the same questions: who's most impacted, who's not speaking, what's being missed, and who can help you address it. The answers are usually already in the room. The coaching is how you hear them.

Accountability that moves things

How it works

Every session moves between four modes — automatically, without Sara needing to name what she needs. This is what makes it different from a psychology session or a strategy workshop. The work moves through the work.

Move

Something's happening. Reduce cognitive load. Find the one thread to pull. The work starts here — with what's actually in the way right now, not what's on the agenda.

See

Something's true that hasn't been fully looked at. Sara holds the pattern without judgment. You're always the authority on your own experience. This is the mode that changes what you can see from where you're standing.

Decide

You already know. The session helps you trust it — names the tension, witnesses the decision so it doesn't get unmade by anxiety later. The decision is marked. It holds.

Link

The tangent is the thing. The connection that looks like a distraction is usually where the real insight lives. This is the mode that ND brains are built for — and what most coaching processes miss entirely.

Common questions

What does a session actually involve?

Fortnightly 1:1 sessions that move between four modes — reducing cognitive load, surfacing what's already there, helping you trust the decision you already know, and following the connection that looks like a tangent but isn't. It starts with a leadership behavioural profile. The insight happens in the doing.

How long's a coaching engagement?

10 sessions over 5 months, or 20 sessions over 10 months. Both fortnightly. The 20-session package includes a personal growth phase and a team growth phase — this is where the cascade effect happens. Both include quarterly outcomes reviews and accountability between sessions.

Is this coaching, consulting, or therapy?

Coaching. The work happens through the work — not theory, not a psychology session. Each session moves something. You leave with a decision made, a pattern named, or an action committed to. Sara has been coaching since 2012. The methodology is rights-based and has been applied at Fortune 500 level and with first-time founders.
